Leadership

My experience of compassionate leadership.

I have experienced both good and bad leadership. After reading this chapter in hindsight I can see several traits in bad leaders that I have experienced that were at a minimum made worse by a lack of compassion and empathy.

On the other hand I have had great leaders that have been able to inspire employees to perform better and have forged loyalty and trust due to their compassion.

Multiplying goodness meditation.

I found this to be a relaxing form of meditation and helpful for getting into a compassionate frame of mind.

Tonglen meditation.

I found this to be a pretty full on exercise. This combined with the multiplying goodness meditation seem to me to be very powerful tools for building compassion.

How the SCARF model apply to my Phase 0 experience.

I found the idea of the SCARF model very interesting, a while ago I had an insight into myself that relates to at least three of these principles. Several years ago I worked out that work became a lot more enjoyable when I knew or had some control over what I would be working on next. Even though this seems pretty obvious at the time it was a bit of a revelation for me.

I can easily see how these principles can be applied on a personal level to a program like EDA in the following ways:

Status comes from bettering ourselves

Certainty comes in the form of clear expectations and goals for projects and assignments

Autonomy in being able to mould our own learning.

Relatedness in meeting new like minded people

Fairness in terms of participating in and open honest and fair organisation.
