What's your take on the DBC/EDA experence?

The EDA course is an immersive leaning experience that empowers students to learn along with their cohort the tools and skills needed to be proficient developer.

What are your impressions?

My initial impressions is that this course will require more than just learning new technical skills and tools, it will involves a lot more soft skill development than I had expected.

I believe that this will be of a benefit not only to my affectedness as a developer but will also help in transitioning into this new profession.

How do you see yourself engaging with this type of culture?

I am a big believer in the self-driven learning method and can see myself thriving in an environment that requires being self-driven.

I would normally be a reluctant participant the some of the “soft skills” aspect of this course but I can see the value of these skills and will be making an effort to push myself in this area.

Have your expectations of EDA changed? If so, how?

My expectations haven’t changed that much mostly because I didn’t really know what to expect and my expectations were pretty vague.

Are you excited to participate in this kind of learning environment? Does it make you nervous?

I am very excited to begin this course and participate in the EDA learning environment.

It does make me a bit nervous, mostly around giving up my job and launching myself into the unknown but also around how I will fit into this environment.
